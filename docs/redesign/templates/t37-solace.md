## Brand & story

Fictional brand "MIDWAY HOTEL" — a cliffside luxury resort perched over the ocean, rendered in a warm Mediterranean/Amalfi-coast dusk palette. The story being sold is escapism-as-craft: not just a place to sleep but an "art" that is "crafted." The opening establishing shot (see GIF) is a drone-style push toward a lit, terraced hotel built into a rocky coastline at golden hour — warm window lights, glowing amber sconces, calm water reflecting the sunset, soft cloud cover drifting past the cliff. This exterior "postcard" shot dissolves into the interior hero UI: guests are meant to feel they are already standing inside the hotel, looking out through its arched windows at the same sunset they just saw from afar. Voice is hushed-luxury: serif display type, italicized emotional accents ("Art"), sparse copy, generous negative space. The brand promise is condensed into two lines of body copy: "Luxury, serenity, and unforgettable moments overlooking endless coastal horizons" and, in the feature card, "An extraordinary retreat crafted for the world's most discerning travelers." Everything — type pairing, color, motion — is built to read as expensive and unhurried.

## Layout — zone by zone

- Right: **"RESERVE NOW"** — a solid cream/off-white pill button with dark charcoal uppercase text and a small circular arrow icon (right-chevron) inside the pill, matching the CTA style used lower in the hero.

**Hero headline block** (left-aligned, upper-middle-left of canvas, roughly 40% from top):

  - Line 1: "CRAFTING"
  - Line 2: "THE **ART**" — word "ART" set in *italic* and shifted to a dusty-rose/pink color (rest of the headline is white)
  - Line 3: "OF ESCAPE"
  - Full headline read together: **"Crafting the *Art* of Escape"**
- Directly below, a shorter italic serif subhead/body line (smaller size, regular weight, white, slightly reduced opacity): "Luxury, serenity, and unforgettable moments overlooking endless coastal horizons."

**Cinematic background scene** (fills entire hero canvas, behind/around all UI):

- A soft, dark, blurred foreground silhouette (an out-of-focus object — reads as a chair-back or lounge furniture edge) sits center-lower, adding depth-of-field/bokeh to the shot, reinforcing that the "camera" is positioned as if a guest is sitting in the space looking outward.
- Top-right corner: trailing vine/plant foliage silhouette hangs into frame from an unseen ledge above, framing the composition.

**Vertical social/media icon rail** (far right edge, vertically centered, stacked):

  1. Top: a picture/gallery-style icon (frame-like glyph)
  2. Middle: a play/media-style triangular glyph
  3. Bottom: an "X" glyph (social/close icon, reads as the X/Twitter mark)

**Glass feature card + thumbnail** (center-right, lower-middle of hero, floating over the scene):

  - Small-caps eyebrow/heading: **"EXPERIENCE UNPARALLELED"**
  - Body copy beneath in smaller grey/white text: "An extraordinary retreat crafted for the world's most discerning travelers."
  - To the right of the text, embedded within the same card group, a small rectangular thumbnail photo (saturated pink/violet-toned image, reads as a night pool or lit interior shot) — acts as a preview/proof-point image adjacent to the copy.

**Numbered progress indicator** (bottom-right of hero):
- Large bold serif numeral **"01"**, followed by a thin horizontal rule/line, followed by a small numeral **"04"** — i.e. "01——04" pagination style, signaling this hero is slide 1 of a 4-part rotating showcase (Suites/Wellness/Dining/Experiences likely map to slides 2–4).

**Scroll cue** (bottom-left of hero):
- Circular badge with a dashed/dotted outer ring, containing the text "SCROLL DOWN" curved/spaced around the circle's inner arc, with a downward-pointing arrow glyph centered in the middle of the circle.

## Typography

- **Display/headline face**: elegant high-contrast serif (Didone/Bodoni-adjacent style — thin hairline strokes paired with thick vertical stems), set in full caps, tight leading between the three stacked lines. One word per emphasis point ("Art") is switched to a matching-weight *italic* cut of the same serif and recolored to dusty-rose, breaking the all-caps rhythm and signaling "this is the emotional word."
- **Body/subhead face**: the same serif family's italic regular weight, sentence case, smaller size, used for the one-line description under the headline and reused for longer copy blocks (feature card body).
- **UI/nav face**: a separate clean geometric/humanist sans-serif in small caps or uppercase, wide letter-spacing, small point size — used for nav links, "RESERVE NOW", "RESERVE YOUR STAY", "EXPLORE SUITES", "EXPERIENCE UNPARALLELED" eyebrow, and "SCROLL DOWN" — this pairing (ornate serif headline + spartan tracked-out sans UI type) is the core luxury-hospitality typographic signature.
- **Numerals**: bold serif ("01") paired with a thin small serif ("04") for the pagination indicator, echoing the headline serif at contrasting scales.

## Motion — every element

- **Entrance**: The sequence opens on a wide cinematic establishing shot — an aerial/drone-style view of the full hotel property built into a cliffside at dusk (see motion GIF): warm lit windows, terraced stone structure, calm water below, soft clouds drifting laterally across the frame, a slow, subtle push-in/zoom toward the building. This exterior shot plays for the first few seconds before the composition transitions into the interior arched-window hero panel.
- **Ambient / idle loop (clouds/light drift)**: Throughout both the establishing shot and the settled hero state, clouds continuously drift slowly across the sunset sky (soft, low-amplitude horizontal motion); the amber wall-sconce glow gently pulses/breathes; the ocean's sunset reflection subtly shimmers. This ambient background animation never fully stops — it is the "living postcard" layer behind the static UI text.
- **Transitions**: The cut from exterior establishing shot to interior hero UI reads as a dissolve/cross-fade — the warm color palette and lighting direction are matched between the two shots so the transition feels continuous rather than jarring (same sunset, same warmth, camera now "inside" looking out).
- **Scroll / parallax / 3D PERSPECTIVE TILT-REVEAL of the hero panel**: The signature move of this template. The entire hero panel (nav + arches + headline + CTAs + card + indicator — i.e. the whole "screen") is first presented tilted in 3D space, as if a rectangular card is angled back and rotated on both X and Y axes relative to the viewer (visible in the angled reference frame: the panel's left edge is lower/closer, the right edge recedes up and away, giving a skewed trapezoidal perspective with the vanishing point off to the upper right). Over the course of roughly a second or two, the panel rotates/untilts — straightening on both axes — until it lands perfectly flat and full-frontal to the viewer (the second reference frame: a clean, straight-on rectangular hero with no skew). This is a single continuous 3D perspective correction: angled/skewed card → smooth rotation → flat, centered, straight-on hero. It plays once per view/loop as the reveal mechanism, not as a scroll-linked effect.
- **Per-element motion (ambient color-bleed backdrop)**: Outside the hero canvas itself, the entire page background (visible in both reference frames as the large surrounding area) is a heavily blurred, oversized crop of the same warm dusty-rose scene — this creates an ambient "color bleed" glow that frames the sharp hero panel, so the warm tones of the hotel scene appear to softly diffuse into the page chrome around it (a common technique: duplicate the hero image, blur it heavily and scale it up, place it behind the crisp panel for atmospheric lighting).
- **Element stagger on reveal**: once the panel is flat/frontal, headline lines, subhead, CTA buttons, feature card, social rail, and the numbered indicator read as having entered with a slight staggered fade/rise (each element settling a beat after the last), typical of this template family's reveal choreography, culminating in the idle state where only clouds/light/reflections continue moving.

## Reusable techniques

1. **Angled-to-flat 3D tilt reveal**: Present the entire hero as a perspective-skewed "card" first, then rotate it to flat/frontal as the primary entrance animation — makes a static screenshot-like hero feel dimensional and cinematic without needing WebGL scroll-jacking.
2. **Blurred, oversized duplicate background for color bleed**: Take the hero's own imagery, blur and upscale it behind the crisp panel to create ambient lighting/atmosphere that matches the scene's palette exactly — cheap, consistent, on-brand glow.
3. **Exterior-to-interior narrative establishing shot**: Open on a wide "where this place is" cinematic shot before dissolving into the "you are already inside" UI shot — builds a sense of place before showing product/navigation.
4. **Two-tier CTA pattern**: One filled pill (cream fill + dark text + colored circular arrow badge) as the primary action, one glass/outline pill as the secondary — consistent sizing, paired horizontally, arrow badge only on primary.
5. **Serif + sans type pairing with a single italic/colored emphasis word**: All-caps display serif headline with exactly one word switched to italic + accent color — a lightweight way to inject emotion/brand color into an otherwise monochrome headline.
6. **Frosted glass feature card with adjacent proof-thumbnail**: Small eyebrow + one-line body copy inside a translucent dark card, paired with a small colored photo thumbnail in the same card group — implies "proof" without a full gallery.
7. **Numbered slide indicator ("01——04")**: Minimal large/small numeral pairing with a connecting rule to signal position within a multi-slide hero/carousel, without needing dots or a visible track.
8. **Circular "SCROLL DOWN" badge with curved text + arrow**: A self-contained scroll affordance that doubles as a decorative brand mark (dashed ring, curved label, centered arrow) rather than a plain chevron.
9. **Vertical icon rail on canvas edge**: Small stacked circular glass icon buttons along the far edge of the hero, keeping secondary/social actions out of the primary reading path while still visible.

## Autari adaptation

Autari sells "AI employees across any field," positioned via consultation (no self-serve checkout) — CTA is **"Book a call"** routing to `noor@autari.co.uk`. Swap the warm dusty-rose luxury-hospitality mood for Autari's Dark Ink `#0B0E12` background with Pine `#0E7C66` → Spark `#19D3A2` green glow as the primary accent (optional Violet `#7C5CFF` as a secondary accent for a second AI-employee "role" or feature highlight).

Specific swaps:
- **Background scene**: Replace the arched-window sunset/ocean cinematic shot with an abstract dark environment — e.g. a softly glowing terminal/dashboard interface, a subtle particle/network mesh, or an abstracted "workspace" render — lit with the Pine→Spark green glow instead of amber sconces. Keep the same compositional idea (dark silhouette foreground, glowing light source mid-ground, soft blurred bokeh) but recolor entirely to the green-on-Dark-Ink system; drop the literal architecture/nature elements (arches, vines, water) since they don't fit an AI-employee product.
- **3D tilt-reveal**: Keep this as Autari's signature entrance move — present the hero panel (or a product/dashboard screenshot) angled/skewed in 3D on load, then rotate to flat/frontal. This works especially well to reveal an AI-employee "workspace" or chat UI screenshot, echoing "unveiling" the product.
- **Ambient color-bleed backdrop**: Reuse directly — blur/upscale the green-glow hero art behind the crisp panel so the whole page radiates a subtle Spark-green ambient glow instead of dusty-rose.
- **Headline**: Replace "Crafting the *Art* of Escape" with something like "Hiring the *Future* of Work" or "Deploying *Intelligence* Across Every Team" — keep the exact structure (three stacked caps lines, one italicized/accent-colored word) but italic word recolors to Spark `#19D3A2` instead of dusty-rose. Since Autari likely won't license a Didone luxury serif, this is the one template where an emotional serif headline is worth keeping as our single "premium moment" — reserve it for the hero only, not for the rest of the site's sans-serif UI type.
- **Subhead**: Replace "Luxury, serenity, and unforgettable moments overlooking endless coastal horizons" with a positioning line, e.g. "AI employees for every field — sourced, trained, and managed for you." Keep the italic serif treatment for continuity with the headline.
- **Dual CTA → Autari mapping**: "RESERVE NOW" (nav pill) → **"Book a call"** (cream pill swapped to Spark-green-on-Dark-Ink filled pill, dark-ink text, small circular arrow badge in Violet or Pine). "RESERVE YOUR STAY" (primary hero pill) → **"Book a call"** (same green-filled pill + arrow badge, linking to `mailto:noor@autari.co.uk` or a Calendly-style booking link). "EXPLORE SUITES" (secondary glass pill) → **"See How It Works"** (kept as the translucent/glass outline pill, lower visual weight, no arrow) — this directly reuses the template's two-tier CTA hierarchy (primary commit action + secondary exploratory action).
- **Nav**: Home / Suites / Wellness / Dining / Experiences → Home / Solutions / Industries / How It Works / Pricing (or similar), keeping the same tracked-out small-caps sans-serif nav styling and the pill-button "Book a call" in the top-right nav slot.
- **Feature card + thumbnail**: "EXPERIENCE UNPARALLELED" / "An extraordinary retreat crafted for the world's most discerning travelers." → an eyebrow like "BUILT FOR ANY FIELD" with body copy such as "AI employees configured and consulted for your exact workflow — not a generic bot." Thumbnail swaps from the pink poolside photo to a small screenshot/avatar of an AI employee persona or a metric snippet (e.g. a mini chat bubble or dashboard crop), keeping the frosted dark-glass card treatment as-is (it already matches Dark Ink well).
- **Numbered indicator ("01——04")**: Reuse directly to indicate 4 hero rotations — e.g. cycling through 4 industries/use-cases ("01 Sales / 02 Support / 03 Ops / 04 Research") the same way the hotel cycles Suites/Wellness/Dining/Experiences.
- **SCROLL DOWN badge**: Reuse as-is structurally (dashed ring, curved label, centered arrow) — recolor ring and text to white/grey-on-Dark-Ink with a faint Spark-green tint on hover/idle-pulse instead of the warm-tone version.
- **Vertical social rail**: Repurpose the three-icon rail for Autari's actual social/contact links (e.g. LinkedIn, X/Twitter, email) rather than gallery/play icons, kept in the same glass-circle, right-edge-stacked treatment.
- **Overall restraint**: This template's power is restraint — one accent color, one accent word, generous negative space, a single dimensional flourish (the tilt-reveal). Autari should resist adding Violet everywhere; use Violet sparingly (e.g. only on the secondary CTA arrow or one card accent) so Spark-green remains the dominant, unambiguous primary signal, mirroring how dusty-rose is the template's only accent against white/cream.
