## Brand & story

Fictional brand built around a serene, aquarium/aqua-glass aesthetic. Hero subject is a photoreal betta (Siamese fighting) fish rendered mid-float, fins fully flared, shot against a soft powder-blue gradient background that reads like the inside wall of an aquarium or a still body of water lit from above. The fish sits at the surface line of a reflective plane — its body and fins mirror downward into a rippling "water" reflection beneath it, reinforcing the "floating / submerged" narrative. Brand name is not shown as a wordmark; identity is carried entirely by the headline copy and a small circular/spiral wave-glyph logo top-left (looks like a stylized nautilus/whirlpool icon, thin-line, white).

Story arc told through copy: "FLOATING THROUGH AQUA DREAMS" (primary emotional hook — dreamlike, weightless, underwater) paired with a secondary vertical copy block "SILENCE / MOVES / BENEATH / LIGHT" (poetic fragment reinforcing calm, depth, submerged light). Supporting body copy (small, right-aligned near the glass pill thumbnails): "Designed to evoke calmness through fluid interactions, atmospheric depth, and abstract aquatic movement." This is the template's explicit design thesis — it is a mood/atmosphere-first hero, not a hard product-feature pitch. Overall positioning: a premium, meditative "aqua lifestyle / wellness-tech" product (reads like a smart aquarium, water-feature brand, or calm/ambient device) with a single soft commerce CTA ("Shop Now").

## Typography

- Headline ("FLOATING / THROUGH / AQUA DREAMS") and the vertical fragment ("SILENCE / MOVES / BENEATH / LIGHT"): thin/light-weight, all-uppercase, geometric/humanist sans (grotesk-style, even stroke width, open counters — visually similar to a light-weight Futura/Century Gothic/Poppins-Light class of face). Generous letter-spacing is not extreme (fairly normal tracking) but the thin stroke weight combined with all-caps gives the "airy" quality specified.
- Nav labels ("Flow", "Current", "Depth", "Motion"): small, same thin sans family, sentence-case (not all-caps like the headline), tight tracking, low visual weight so they recede behind the hero image.
- "24k": largest secondary numeral, thin weight, sans — reads almost like a stat/spec callout.
- "Motion Loop": small caption below "24k", slightly italicized or a lighter/condensed cut, sentence-case.
- CTA "Shop Now": small, regular-weight sans, dark color on white pill — the one piece of copy in the whole frame with normal (not ultra-thin) weight and dark-on-light contrast, making it the clear clickable focal point.
- Body copy micro-paragraph (glass pill annotation): smallest size in the hierarchy, thin weight, sentence case, tight line-height, low-contrast white-on-blue.
- No serif or slab elements anywhere; entire type system is a single thin-sans family used at varying sizes/weights/case to build hierarchy purely through scale, case, and opacity rather than mixing typefaces.

## Motion — every element

- **Entrance**: Scene opens on the tilted 3D laptop-mockup framing (device angled in perspective, as captured in the reference GIF) with the aqua hero content already visible inside the screen, softly blurred/out-of-focus surroundings extending past the device edges. This is the "cold open" state of the loop.
- **Ambient / idle loop (subject motion, water ripple)**: While the device sits in/near its resting frontal state, the betta fish performs continuous slow idle motion — fins undulate/ripple outward in a gentle wave (long tail fins trailing and billowing as if suspended in still water), body sways very slightly side to side, consistent with a looping cinemagraph-style ambient animation rather than a one-shot entrance. Simultaneously, the reflection beneath the waterline animates independently with a subtle rippling distortion (wavy horizontal displacement, soft light-caustic shimmer) so the mirrored fish image never sits perfectly still even when the fish above holds a near-static pose. This dual motion (fish fins + water ripple) is the core "living" loop that repeats for the bulk of the 32.3s duration.
- **Transitions**: Copy blocks (headline, "SILENCE/MOVES/BENEATH/LIGHT" fragment, "24k Motion Loop" badge, micro-copy paragraph) appear to fade/build in with staggered opacity — evidenced by "MOVES" being captured mid-fade (visibly dimmer than its neighboring lines "SILENCE/BENEATH/LIGHT" in the same frame), implying a word-by-word or line-by-line staggered fade-up rather than all text appearing simultaneously. The CTA pill and nav are held at full opacity throughout (chrome/UI elements, not part of the staggered copy reveal).
- **Scroll / parallax / 3D perspective TILT reveal**: The signature transition of this template is a 3D-perspective device tilt: the hero panel begins angled away from the viewer (skewed/rotated on the X and Y axes, as if the laptop screen is being viewed from an off-center vantage — visible in the motion GIF as a rhomboid/keystoned framing of the same content that reads as a flat rectangle in the full-size frames), then animates toward the camera, de-rotating/straightening until the panel sits flat and frontal, filling the viewport edge-to-edge (matching `t21-a.jpeg`). During this de-tilt, the background outside the device frame (the same blue-grey aqua gradient, blurred) provides continuity so the transition reads as "zooming/settling into" the screen rather than a hard cut. A companion pan/zoom is also present: `t21-b.jpeg` is a tighter, slightly right-shifted crop of the same composition (nav row sits higher and closer to the top edge, headline is cropped at the left edge mid-word, "24k Motion Loop" badge and fish reflection are enlarged relative to frame) — indicating the camera also slowly pushes in / pans across the hero as part of the loop, independent of the tilt-to-flat move.
- **Per-element motion (radial motion-loop badge)**: The "24k Motion Loop" dot-burst radiates outward from the center in concentric pulses — dots appear to animate with a gentle staggered opacity/scale pulse traveling from the innermost ring to the outermost (a "sonar ping" or breathing radial animation), looping continuously and independent of the fish/water animation, functioning as a persistent ambient "this is a live motion loop" indicator rather than a one-time reveal.
- **CTA pill**: Static positionally but implied hover/idle affordance via the embedded circular arrow button (typical of this pill-CTA pattern: subtle scale/shift on hover, arrow nudges right) — no direct hover frame captured, noted as the expected interactive behavior for this component type.
- **Nav**: Active item "Flow" is held bright/white while other three items stay dimmed for the full duration of the captured frames — no evidence of a nav-hover color swap in the static captures, but the bright/dim split is consistent with a standard active-state nav treatment.

## Reusable techniques

- Full-viewport hero photo/render as the entire background, with all UI (nav, copy, CTA) laid directly over it using opacity/weight contrast for hierarchy instead of solid color panels or scrims.
- "Surface + reflection" split-frame trick: place the hero subject at a horizontal waterline and mirror/distort a second copy of it below to instantly read as "liquid/glass surface" — cheap to fake with a flipped, blurred, rippled duplicate layer and a gradient fade.
- Thin-weight, all-caps, multi-line stacked headline for an airy/premium feel — avoids bulky type competing with a detailed photographic subject.
- Radial dot-burst "stat/spec badge" (here "24k Motion Loop") as a decorative-but-informative device: communicates a technical spec (resolution/loop count) while doubling as ambient background texture/animation.
- Single pill-shaped CTA with embedded circular icon-button at one end — compact, thumb-friendly, universally recognizable "go" affordance; only high-contrast (dark-on-white) element in an otherwise low-contrast, all-white-on-color composition.
- Secondary vertical word-stack ("SILENCE / MOVES / BENEATH / LIGHT") used as atmospheric flavor copy distinct from the primary headline — gives a second read layer for users who linger, without competing with the primary CTA path.
- Small glass/capsule-shaped thumbnail swatches paired with a short design-thesis sentence — a lightweight way to gesture at "more content/detail exists" (like a mini gallery teaser) without building a full carousel.
- Device-frame (laptop mockup) tilt-to-flat reveal as a scroll-triggered or load-triggered transition — gives the hero a "materializing into place" moment and disguises a static hero as a more dynamic 3D scene.
- Staggered per-line/per-word fade-in for stacked copy blocks to add motion without needing complex choreography — cheap perceived-production-value technique.

## Autari adaptation

Base surface swaps to Dark Ink `#0B0E12` (replacing the light powder-blue-grey background) with the glow duo Pine `#0E7C66` → Spark `#19D3A2` standing in for the aqua/coral palette as the primary accent gradient (used on the CTA glow, the radial badge dots, and any "liquid/glass" fluid-texture swatches — where t21 used emerald-green and magenta-pink glass pills, Autari uses a Pine→Spark green pill plus an optional Violet `#7C5CFF` pill for a secondary accent, keeping the same "two contrasting glass capsules" layout but recolored to brand). Headline typography keeps the thin/airy uppercase geometric-sans treatment but the copy itself repositions from mood ("FLOATING THROUGH AQUA DREAMS") to capability + reach, e.g. a 3-line stack such as "HIRE AI EMPLOYEES / FOR ANY FIELD / ON DEMAND" — same thin-weight, all-caps, left-aligned, staggered fade-in-by-line pattern as the reference.

The hero "subject" swap: replace the photoreal betta fish with an abstract AI-agent visual motif — e.g., a glowing Pine/Spark particle-mesh humanoid silhouette, an animated neural/agent avatar, or a glass-morphic orb/network graphic — placed in the same right-of-center, large-scale hero position, with the same "surface + reflection" trick reused (mirror the glowing agent shape into a dim rippling reflection beneath a horizontal divider) to keep the calm/glass mood while trading "water" for a dark reflective floor/glass-desk surface consistent with the Dark Ink theme.

The radial-dotted "24k Motion Loop" badge maps directly to Autari's "24/7 always-on" badge: keep the identical concentric dot-burst radial animation (ping/sonar pulse) but recolor the dots from white to Spark `#19D3A2` (or white at reduced opacity over the dark background) and swap the copy to "24/7" (large numeral, thin weight) stacked above "Always-On Agents" (small caption), preserving the exact "big stat + small caption inside a radiating dot ring" composition and its ambient looping animation.

CTA pill keeps the same shape/position/affordance (label + embedded circular arrow button) but swaps "Shop Now" for "Book a call", linking to `mailto:noor@autari.co.uk` (or a booking link if later added); pill fill can stay white (dark text) to preserve max-contrast "the one clickable thing" effect against the dark hero, matching the reference's high-contrast pill against its light hero.

The secondary vertical word-stack ("SILENCE / MOVES / BENEATH / LIGHT") maps to a short benefit fragment reinforcing the positioning, e.g. "TRAINED / ON YOUR / BUSINESS / CONTEXT" or "AGENTS / ACROSS / ANY / FIELD" — same 4-line thin uppercase stack, same staggered fade, same lower-right placement.

The glassy calm of t21 (soft aqua gradients, translucent capsule thumbnails, low-chroma background against one vivid accent subject) maps to Autari's glassmorphism system directly: frosted/blurred glass panel surfaces at low opacity over Dark Ink, Pine/Spark glow used sparingly (on the fish-equivalent hero subject, the badge, and the CTA hover state) exactly as t21 uses saturated color sparingly (fish + two glass pills) against an otherwise monochrome aqua field — same "one saturated hero + one saturated accent duo + everything else desaturated" color discipline, just remapped from aqua/coral to Pine/Spark(/Violet) on black instead of on light blue-grey.

Nav bar structure (centered pill-style label group + far-right icon cluster of search/account/menu-grid) is reusable as-is for Autari's top nav, with the 4 labels renamed to reflect Autari's own sections (e.g., "Product · Solutions · Pricing · About") in place of "Flow / Current / Depth / Motion", keeping the same active-vs-dimmed brightness contrast to indicate current section — swap active-white to active-Spark-green for on-brand emphasis if a persistent accent color is desired in the nav.

Device-frame tilt-to-flat reveal technique is directly reusable for Autari's hero load/scroll transition: open on a slightly tilted 3D glass panel (consistent with a "glass device" motif) that settles flat as the page loads or as the user scrolls past the initial viewport, reinforcing the glassmorphic, dimensional feel without needing custom 3D asset work beyond a CSS/WebGL perspective transform on the existing hero panel.
