## Brand & story
Fictional brand: **"vegtopia"** — a plant-based / vegan sandwich and food brand. Logo mark: a small circular badge (rounded-square/circle icon, green) followed by the wordmark "vegtopia" in lowercase, clean rounded sans-serif, dark ink color, sitting top-left of the nav bar next to a small square icon tile.

Story/positioning: healthy plant-based eating reframed as bold and exciting rather than bland — literally spelled out in the hero headline "GREEN NOT BORING." Supporting copy: *"Vegtopia transforms healthy eating into an exciting experience filled with crispy layers, smoky flavors, and satisfying plant-based creations."* The hero product is a towering, dripping deli sandwich shot as editorial food photography (torn wheat bread, lettuce, tomato, red onion, herbs, visible drip/condensation droplets), positioned as if levitating/falling into frame — high-energy food-editorial style rather than clinical product photography.

## Typography
- **Display/headline**: oversized, all-caps, hand-stamped/distressed poster face with rough stencil-paint texture on the edges of every letterform — reads like a rubber-stamped or spray-stencil grunge font (poster/editorial-street style, not a clean grotesk). Used only for "GREEN NOT BORING."
- **Nav / body / UI**: clean, rounded, geometric-humanist sans-serif (lowercase logotype "vegtopia", nav links in sentence case, subheadline copy, card product names and descriptions) — legible, friendly, low-contrast strokes; consistent with brands like Poppins/General Sans/Circular in spirit.
- **Card/price micro-type**: small sans-serif, medium weight for product names, regular weight for description lines, price set apart in a teal/green tint.
- **Seal-badge type**: small caps or uppercase condensed sans, curved/set on a circular baseline path around the ring ("PURE PLANT POWER").

## Motion — every element
- **Entrance**: the vertical canvas opens on the empty turquoise field with the iMac silhouette fading/scaling in first (device mockup establishes). The on-screen web content then builds up inside the iMac screen: nav bar and logo fade in first (top-left "vegtopia" wordmark appears), followed by the sandwich hero image **dropping in from off-screen top**, then the stamped headline "GREEN NOT BORING" stamps/punches into place (consistent with its rubber-stamp texture — reads as if slammed down rather than faded), then the subheadline and "Add to cart" pill fade/slide up last, and the product-card carousel slides in from the right after the headline settles.
- **Ambient / idle loop**: continuous small **sauce/condensation droplets** fall and drift beneath the sandwich the entire time (looping particle/drip effect) — the primary "always alive" ambient cue. The sandwich itself has a very subtle float/bob. The teal background gradient is static (no animated gradient shift observed).
- **Transitions (dramatic zoom)**: the camera performs a **push-in / crash-zoom** from the wide "full iMac mockup" framing (device bezel fully visible, entire hero composition visible — see full establishing frame) toward a **tight cropped close-up** on the upper-right portion of the screen (sandwich + nav, device bezel no longer visible, image cropped at both top and left edges) — this is the "dramatic zoom transition" called out for this template: it moves the viewer from "here's the whole website in a device" to "here's the content up close," simulating a camera diving into the screen. Mid-zoom frames show partial/cropped letterforms and UI elements sliding past the frame edges, confirming a continuous camera move rather than a hard cut.
- **Scroll / parallax / 3D tilt**: no independent page-scroll or 3D-tilt of the panel is evidenced (unlike T21/T22/T28/T37's tilt-reveals) — the "movement" in this template is carried entirely by the camera zoom described above plus the internal carousel/seal motion, not by the mockup itself rotating or tilting in 3D space.
- **Per-element motion**:
  - **Carousel**: product cards auto-advance/slide horizontally (filmstrip drifts leftward continuously, revealing new cards from the right, consistent with the extra card sliver cropped at the frame's right edge); the small circular ◀ ▶ arrows imply the carousel is also manually steppable.
  - **Seal rotation**: the circular "PURE PLANT POWER" badge **slowly rotates in place** (continuous, slow clockwise spin — the "seal never stops spinning" ambient touch), while the center "V" leaf mark stays upright/static (a common seal-badge technique: ring spins, center icon does not).
  - **Nav**: nav pill itself is static/pinned, but the active-state highlight ("Home" pill) and the cart notification dot draw subtle attention; no visible hover/press states captured, nav treated as ambient-still relative to the more energetic hero elements.
  - **Droplets**: individual sauce/condensation droplets fall at staggered rates and fade out near the bottom of the visible hero area — a particle-style loop rather than a single one-shot drip.

## Reusable techniques
1. **Device-in-context presentation** — the whole hero is staged inside an iMac mockup whose background color matches the site's own background, so the "frame" visually disappears into the page (steal for a "see it live" or process section).
2. **Camera push-in / crash-zoom transition** — moving from "full device shot" to "cropped close-up on content" as a scene transition, rather than a cut or simple fade.
3. **Rubber-stamp/distressed two-tone headline** — one giant word stamped in the accent color inside an otherwise cream/white headline, for a single emphasized word without changing font or weight.
4. **Rotating circular seal badge** — dashed-ring badge with repeating small-caps text around the edge and a static center icon, continuously spinning — an "authenticity/quality" motif that can double as a "trusted by" or "always-on" badge.
5. **Product/service card carousel with circular nav arrows** — glass/frosted rounded cards (icon-thumbnail + name + one-line description + price/value), auto-scrolling filmstrip with manual ◀ ▶ override.
6. **Falling-ingredient / particle drip ambient loop** — small looping particles (droplets here) that keep a hero photo feeling "alive" without animating the photo itself.
7. **Pill nav with an active-tab micro-pill** — the current nav item gets its own small colored capsule nested inside the larger nav pill, cheap and clear way to show "you are here."
8. **Vertical icon rail** — thin column of circular social icons pinned to a screen edge, independent of the main nav.
9. **Dual CTA (filled pill + icon-only secondary)** — "Add to cart" pill paired with a small circular icon-only button for a quick-action variant of the same intent.
