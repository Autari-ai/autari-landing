## Brand & story

This is a "dreamy dark fantasy" demo hero built around a two-act narrative told through a single continuous camera/scroll move:

1. **Act 1 — "Step Into Wonder."** A near-black, moody woodland/garden at dusk — silhouetted branches, wild lavender flowers, a warm amber lantern-glow bleeding through the undergrowth. A glowing oval portal opens in the foliage revealing a pastel sky beyond. This is the "threshold" moment — an invitation, mystery, low light, high contrast.
2. **Act 2 — "Create Beyond Reality."** The portal is passed through; the viewer is now *above the clouds* in a soft pastel dawn/dusk sky. The mood flips from mysterious/dark to airy/bright. Floating tag/label cards on a signpost hover in the cloud field, implying a curated collection of possibilities on the other side of the portal.

Story arc = **mystery/threshold → payoff/wonderland**. The whole template is a metaphor for "cross a portal and the ordinary becomes extraordinary" — an editing/creation tool (this is a demo for an AI photo/video editor, "edit hero lp templates") pitching itself as a doorway to unreal, beautiful output. The brand voice is soft, editorial, aspirational, slightly magical-realist — serif type, muted pastel/jewel palette, nothing neon or techy.

## Layout — zone by zone

**Scene 1 (0s–8s) — "Step Into Wonder" / the portal garden**
- Full-bleed background: dark garden photography, warm amber glow bottom-right, purple/lavender wildflower silhouettes bottom-left, dark tree canopy top.
- Center-right of frame: a large glowing **oval/arched portal** cut into the canopy, showing the pastel sky of Act 2 through it — this is the focal element the whole composition is built around (off-center, roughly right-of-center, occupying ~35% of frame height).
- Top-left corner: small circular badge/rosette icon (star-flower mark) — a persistent logo/watermark chip present in both scenes.
- Left-of-center, vertically centered: headline block —
  - Headline: **"STEP INTO WONDER"** set in elegant all-caps serif, left-aligned, broken across two lines in the captured frames as **"STEP • INTO" / "WONDER"** (a small centered dot/bullet glyph sits between "STEP" and "INTO" as a decorative divider — not punctuation, a design flourish).
  - Below the headline: a short paragraph of body copy (placeholder/lorem-style microcopy, illegible at capture resolution — treat as a 1–2 sentence sub-headline slot, small sans-serif, muted off-white, left-aligned, max-width ~30–35% of frame).
- Right side, floating over/beside the portal: a **fanned stack of 2–3 small "glass" thumbnail cards** — rounded-square frosted/glass cards containing photo thumbnails (portrait/landscape imagery) with tiny icon badges (star/heart) pinned to a corner of each card. Cards are stacked at slightly different rotations (fan/scatter arrangement), overlapping.
- Bottom-center: a row of small **pagination/progress dots** (carousel indicator style) that, between ~6s–8s, morphs/grows into a **circular "Watch Demo" button** (a play-triangle icon inside a circle) — this is the scroll/interaction cue signaling more content or a demo video is available.
- 8s: **transition frame** — headline's trailing letters ("…ER" of WONDER) slide/wipe off the left edge while Act 2's cloud-sky scene slides/wipes in from the right, carrying a fully-formed pill-shaped **"Watch Demo"** button (play icon + label text) into view alongside the new floating cards.

**Scene 2 (8s–19s+) — "Create Beyond Reality" / above the clouds**
- Full-bleed background: pastel sunset/dawn cloud-scape (peach → mauve-pink → lavender-blue gradient sky, soft cumulus clouds lit from within).
- Top-center: headline **"CREATE BEYOND REALITY"**, single line, elegant all-caps serif, centered, with a short centered sub-paragraph of body copy beneath (placeholder microcopy, illegible at capture res — same treatment as Act 1's sub-headline).
- Top-left corner: same small circular star/rosette badge/logo chip carried over from Scene 1 (brand-consistency element across both acts).
- Center-lower, "planted" into the cloud bank like a signpost: a **cluster of 4 small square "tag/label" cards** (mint green, lavender/purple, sky blue, pale yellow) — styled like plant-marker tags or luggage labels, each attached to a thin post/stem, fanned out and tilted at different angles, floating just above the cloud horizon line. Micro-copy on each tag is present but illegible at capture resolution (short 2-line labels, e.g. category/feature names).
- A small dot indicator (soft green) sits near the bottom of the composition, likely an interaction affordance (progress/scroll marker) echoing the dot-cue from Scene 1.
- This scene holds for the remainder of the clip (8s→19s) with the tag-card cluster in continuous slow ambient motion (see Motion section) rather than any further hard cuts — it is the "resting"/loop state of the hero.

## Typography

- **Display/headline**: elegant, centered-or-left, **all-caps serif** with generous letter-spacing (tracking). Thin-to-medium weight strokes, high-contrast serif (thick/thin stroke contrast reminiscent of editorial/fashion serifs like Canela, Reckless, or GT Sectra). Used for both "STEP INTO WONDER" and "CREATE BEYOND REALITY" — same typeface/treatment in both acts, reinforcing the single-story continuity.
- Scene 1 headline is **left-aligned** (paired with body copy underneath, forming a left-stacked text block); Scene 2 headline is **centered** (paired with centered body copy) — the alignment shift from left→center visually marks the "arrival"/settling from journey (Act 1) to destination (Act 2).
- **Body/sub-headline copy**: small, sans-serif, light weight, muted off-white/cream, low-contrast against background (intentionally secondary to the serif display headline). Placeholder/lorem content in the source — treat as a 1–2 line supporting sentence slot only.
- **Button label** ("Watch Demo"): sans-serif, medium weight, small caps or sentence case, paired with a play-triangle glyph inside a circular/pill container.
- No visible numerals, no monospace, no heavy/bold display anywhere — the whole type system stays light-touch and airy to match the "dream" mood.

## Motion — every element

- **Entrance**: Cold open already inside the dark garden (0s) — no fade-from-black. At ~1s the canopy gap widens/reveals to hint at the portal. At ~2s the full portal, headline, body copy, and floating glass cards all appear to be already resolved/settled — i.e., the entrance happens in the first ~2s as a quick reveal (portal aperture opening + text/card fade-and-rise-in), then the composition holds.
- **Ambient / idle loop** (the signature "living" quality of this template — nothing is ever fully static):
  - The **glowing portal** has a slow **breathing pulse** — its rim-light glow subtly brightens/dims and its edge softly shimmers, like a light source with atmospheric flicker (candle/lantern quality, not a hard strobe).
  - Fine **light motes/particles** drift upward and sideways through the garden scene (dust/pollen/firefly-like specks catching the amber glow), giving the dark scene depth and life even though the camera doesn't move.
  - The **floating glass thumbnail cards** (Scene 1) and the **floating tag/label cards** (Scene 2) are in **continuous, slow, physics-y ambient motion** — they never lock into a static fanned pose. Across the contact-sheet timeline (compare 8s→12s→16s→19s) the same 4 tag cards visibly **reshuffle their left-right order, rotation angle, and vertical offset** frame over frame: e.g. the mint-green card is left-most and tilted one way at 8s, then by 13s the lavender card has drifted to a different position and tilt, by 17s the arrangement has reshuffled again. This reads as gentle independent bobbing/drifting per card — each card moves on its own loose rotation + vertical float (like tags on strings swaying in a breeze), asynchronous from its neighbors, so the cluster is always subtly "alive" and rearranging rather than holding a fixed pose. Same logic applies to the 2–3 glass cards in Scene 1, which visibly shift position/rotation between every captured second.
  - The pagination dots at the bottom of Scene 1 pulse/cycle (indicating carousel progress) before morphing into the Watch Demo button around 6s–8s.
- **Transitions** (scene cross-fade/wipe between the two worlds, ~8s mark): a **horizontal slide-wipe**, not a simple opacity cross-fade — Scene 1's headline visibly exits by sliding/clipping off the **left** edge (captured mid-motion as the trailing "…ER" of WONDER) while Scene 2's cloud background and its Watch Demo button + cards slide in from the **right**, overlapping briefly. This reads as a horizontal panel/carousel-style transition (consistent with the "scroll/swipe to travel through the portal" narrative) rather than a dissolve.
- **Scroll / parallax**: the bottom-center dot indicator and the emergent Watch Demo button function as the scroll/interaction cue — they signal "more below" and likely drive a scroll-linked or click-triggered transition into Scene 2. Within each scene, the background (garden / cloud-scape) likely has a slow, near-imperceptible parallax drift (the light motes and cloud texture appear to shift slightly independent of the foreground text/card layers) — foreground UI (headline, cards, button) stays locked to the viewport while background imagery breathes/drifts behind it.
- **Per-element motion summary**:
  - Portal: slow pulse/breathe (glow intensity + soft edge shimmer), constant.
  - Light motes/particles: continuous slow upward/lateral drift, constant, throughout Scene 1.
  - Glass thumbnail cards (Scene 1): continuous slow drift + rotation + re-stacking, asynchronous per card.
  - Tag/label cards (Scene 2): continuous slow drift + rotation + reshuffling of stacking order, asynchronous per card, strongest/most visible "alive" motion in the whole template.
  - Headline text: static once settled within a scene; slides off/on only at the scene transition.
  - Watch Demo button: grows out of the dot-indicator (scale/morph reveal) around 6s–8s, then sits static (aside from perhaps a subtle hover/pulse not distinguishable at this capture rate).
  - Badge/logo chip (top-left): static, persistent across both scenes (anchors brand identity through the transition).

## Reusable techniques

1. **Threshold/portal focal element** — a single glowing aperture as the visual "center of gravity" of the hero, with the rest of the composition (dark foreground, text, cards) arranged around it. Strong technique for "gateway to a product/experience" positioning.
2. **Two-act color-mood flip** — same layout skeleton (headline + subcopy + floating cards + badge), but the palette flips from dark/moody/warm-accent to bright/airy/pastel-multicolor between acts, communicating a before/after or journey/destination narrative without changing the underlying grid.
3. **Perpetual-motion floating cards** — never let secondary UI elements (thumbnail/tag cards) go fully static; give each one its own slow, slightly-offset drift/rotation loop so the whole card cluster reads as "alive" and dimensional (parallax-like) even on a still hero.
4. **Dot-indicator-morphs-into-CTA** — reuse the same small circular affordance for both "carousel progress dot" and "watch demo" button by having one grow/expand into the other, tying passive pagination UI and the primary CTA into a single visual object instead of two separate widgets.
5. **Horizontal wipe scene transition** tied to scroll/interaction, reinforcing the "step through" narrative literally (text and scene both move in the same direction, like walking forward through a doorway).
6. **Consistent corner badge across scene changes** — a small static logo/rosette chip that never moves or transitions, anchoring brand presence while everything else around it transforms.

## Autari adaptation

**Palette swap**: Replace the dark-garden black/amber with **Dark Ink `#0B0E12`** as the base background (both acts can share this single dark tone — Autari doesn't need the light pastel "Act 2" sky; instead treat both acts as dark-mode, differentiated by glow intensity/color rather than by flipping to a pastel sky). Replace the amber lantern-glow with **Pine `#0E7C66` → Spark `#19D3A2`** as the primary glow gradient (this becomes the "portal" light source — an energy-core glow instead of a lantern-glow). Reserve **Violet `#7C5CFF`** as a secondary accent for a second card category or a hover/active state, echoing the lavender flowers' role as a secondary hue in the original without introducing a full pastel-sky palette shift.

**Positioning swap**: "Step Into Wonder" → "Create Beyond Reality" maps directly onto Autari's pitch of **"AI employees across ANY field, sold via consultation."** Suggested headline pairing:
- Act 1 (mystery/threshold): something like **"MEET YOUR AI WORKFORCE"** — dark scene, single glowing energy-core portal as the focal element (this is where the Spark-green glow breathing/pulsing lives), floating glass cards on the right showing 2–3 rotating "employee role" thumbnails (e.g., "Support Agent," "Sales Rep," "Ops Analyst" — swap the photo-thumbnail glass cards for role-preview glass cards).
- Act 2 (payoff): something like **"BUILT FOR ANY FIELD"** or **"WORK BEYOND LIMITS"** — same dark background (skip the literal pastel-cloud swap; keep Dark Ink throughout for brand consistency), with the floating tag-card cluster re-purposed as **floating glass stat cards** (e.g., "40 hrs saved/week," "24/7 coverage," "Any industry") on thin sign-post-style connectors, still tinted with green/violet glass tones rather than pastel mint/lavender/blue/yellow.

**The living card-motion steal (the most important technique to port)**: The template's key "wow" isn't the portal alone, it's that the floating cards **never stop drifting** — each one independently bobs, rotates a few degrees, and slowly reshuffles its stacking order against its neighbors, on a loose, staggered, asynchronous loop (confirmed by comparing the 8s/13s/17s frames where the same 4 cards visibly swap positions/tilts). Apply this directly to Autari's floating glass stat cards: give each card its own randomized-looking animation loop — e.g. `translateY` oscillating ±6–10px over 4–7s (per-card randomized duration/phase so they never sync), a slight `rotate` wobble of ±2–4deg, and an occasional z-order/position swap with a neighboring card every 6–10s using an eased cross-move (not a hard cut). Use CSS `@keyframes` with `animation-delay` staggered per card (or Framer Motion `useAnimationFrame`/`animate` with per-card random seeds) so the cluster reads as gently alive rather than looping in visible lockstep.

**The glowing focal element steal**: Port the portal's slow breathing-glow technique directly onto Autari's green energy-core/orb — a radial gradient (`Pine #0E7C66` core → `Spark #19D3A2` rim → transparent) with a slow (~4–6s) opacity/scale pulse plus a few drifting particle motes rising through it (small blurred dots in Spark green, opacity-faded, slow upward drift with slight horizontal sway) — this recreates the "dust motes catching the lantern glow" ambiance from Scene 1 using the brand's green instead of amber.

**CTA/button swap**: "Watch Demo" (play-triangle + pill button, morphing out of a dot-indicator) → **"See how it works"** as the equivalent secondary affordance (same morph-from-dot-indicator technique, same play-icon-in-circle visual language, just re-labeled). Primary conversion CTA becomes **"Book a call"**, routed to `noor@autari.co.uk` — position this as the primary button near the headline (where the original template has no hard-sell CTA, only "Watch Demo"), with "See how it works" demoted to a secondary/ghost button beside it, matching the template's soft, non-pushy invitation tone but adding the commercial CTA Autari actually needs.

**Transition swap**: Keep the horizontal wipe-transition technique for a second hero panel/section (e.g., wiping from "Meet Your AI Workforce" into "Built for Any Field") if Autari's hero is a two-panel scroll/slide story; otherwise this technique can be simplified to a single static hero with only the ambient card/glow motion (skip the scene-change wipe) if a one-screen hero is preferred — the card-drift and glow-pulse are the higher-value techniques to keep regardless.
