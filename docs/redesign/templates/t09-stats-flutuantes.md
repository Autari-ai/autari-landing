## Brand & story
No persistent brand wordmark is legible anywhere in the capture — this is a generic multi-SKU smoothie/juice product template whose "identity" is carried entirely by three punning flavor wordmarks that take turns filling the screen: **"Strawlicious"** (strawberry), **"Graptastical"** (grape), and **"Kiwliciously"** (kiwi — note the odd adverb form, inconsistent with the other two adjective-style puns, but that is what the source renders). A small top-left nav mark (an upward chevron/droplet glyph + a short ~6–8 letter wordmark) is present in every frame but is rendered at native pixel heights far below legibility — it reads as "present" rather than "readable," so treat it as a placeholder logo slot, not a specific name.

The story being sold is "one juice bar, three (or more) rotating flavors" — a product carousel disguised as a single hero shot. Each flavor gets: its own giant name-pun wordmark, its own smoothie-glass hero photo (product shot with a mid-air splash and whole/halved fruit pieces suspended around it), its own full-bleed color wash (bg + card + even the wordmark's off-white fill all re-tint to match), and its own small info panel (thumbnail + heading + 2-line copy + CTA). The three adjective/adverb tags **"Fresh," "Sweet," "Irresistible"** sit above the wordmark as the hero's persistent brand-voice line — these read as the closest thing to the "stat chips" the template family name promises, though here they are qualitative descriptor pills rather than numeric stats (see note in Layout).

## Typography
- **Giant flavor wordmark**: extremely bold, tightly-set, condensed grotesque/sans display face, mixed-case but reading as a single fused compound word (no space) — the single loudest element in the composition by a wide margin, sized to nearly fill the card width.
- **Feature-tag chips ("Fresh," "Sweet," "Irresistible")**: light/regular weight, small size, sentence-case (not all-caps), sitting inside thin pill outlines — a much quieter, softer voice than the wordmark, meant to read as friendly copy rather than shouting UI.
- **Nav / info-panel text**: small, light-weight sans, consistent with the chip typeface family — a single supporting type voice used for every secondary UI element (nav links, panel heading, panel caption, CTA labels), none of it large enough to compete with the wordmark.
- Overall type voice: one heavy condensed display face for the "hero word," one light general-purpose sans for absolutely everything else — a two-weight system, not a multi-face one.

## Motion — every element
Reconstructed from the second-by-second contact sheet (0s–17s) plus the GIF's captured idle moment. The sequence is a **crash-zoom + color-theme carousel**, and — critically — it is a **palindrome loop**: Strawberry → Grape → Kiwi → Grape → Strawberry, which is what lets 17.4s of forward playback loop back to its own starting frame with no visible cut.

- **Entrance (crash-zoom into the wordmark)**: the loop opens (0s–1s) on a medium-zoom view of "Strawlicious" — tight enough that the full card chrome (nav, chips, carousel arrows) is NOT yet visible, but loose enough that the whole word plus its splash-glass graphic and both floating strawberries are in frame. From here the camera keeps pushing in (1s→2s) until the frame is cropped to show only the wordmark's tail ("cious") filling most of the width — at this tighter crop, the card's edges finally come into view and the chrome (nav strip, "Fresh/Sweet/Irresistible" chips, carousel arrows) fades/settles in around the now off-center text. This crash-zoom-while-revealing-chrome move is the core entrance technique: the "product moment" (word + splash + fruit) is established first, then the "site chrome" resolves in around it as the camera keeps tightening.
- **Settled hold (2s–4s, strawberry only in this capture)**: once chrome has fully resolved, the frame holds near-static at this tight crop for ~3 seconds — this is the only point in the loop where every UI layer (nav, chips, wordmark tail, carousel arrows) is simultaneously visible and legible. The splash graphic and floating strawberries continue their own idle motion underneath (see next bullet) even while the "camera" is otherwise still.
- **Ambient / idle loop (splash video, chip float/bob)**: even during the "held" beats, nothing is a frozen frame — the smoothie-glass splash graphic behind/through the wordmark is a looping micro-video (liquid crown constantly rippling, never settling), and the whole and halved fruit pieces scattered around the word visibly drift/bob at slightly different phases and speeds (a soft floating motion, matching the "flutuantes" name) rather than holding a fixed position. The diamond sparkle particles likewise drift rather than sit static.
- **Transitions (color-theme crossfades between flavors)**: at each flavor handoff (5s, 8s, 11s, 14s) three things move together as one graded crossfade, not a hard cut: (1) the ambient outer wash and card interior both ramp their hue from the outgoing flavor to the incoming one (visible mid-transition as a muddy blend — e.g. 11s reads as an olive-brown mix of kiwi-green bleeding into grape-purple); (2) the wordmark itself crossfades letter-for-letter into the new flavor's word while the camera is mid-zoom, producing brief overlapping-text frames (5s shows "ious Graptas" — the tail of "Strawlicious" ghosting against the head of "Graptastical"; 14s shows "licious Grapt" — the reverse handoff, with loose strawberries and diamonds visibly falling/drifting into frame as the new fruit's photography starts layering in); (3) the splash-glass hero photo and its orbiting fruit swap wholesale to the new flavor's assets (strawberries out, grapes in, etc.) within the same 1-second transition window, timed to land just as the new wordmark becomes fully legible.
- **Scroll / parallax**: not evidenced — this is a locked single-viewport hero loop with no scroll-driven state change captured anywhere in the 17.4s; all motion is camera zoom + color crossfade + idle ambient drift, not scroll position.
- **Per-element motion (text-behind-splash)**: the signature technique of this template is that the splash-glass photo is composited to render IN FRONT OF the wordmark's middle letters but BEHIND its outer letters (or vice versa depending on the exact letterform), so the giant word never sits as a flat static text layer — it always looks like the smoothie glass has physically splashed up through the type, with liquid droplets and fruit occluding a few letters at a time. Combined with the constant loop-video splash animation, the wordmark reads as "alive" rather than a static PNG overlay, even when the camera itself is holding still.
- **Full per-second map** (for future reference, since this is the permanent record): 0–1s medium-zoom "Straw[glass]cious" reveal → 2–4s tight-zoom settled hold with full chrome (chips/nav/arrows) → 5s crossfade to grape begins ("ious Graptas") → 6–7s tight-zoom "Grap[glass]stical" hold (full card visible, no chip row shown at this crop depth) → 8s crossfade to kiwi begins ("l Kiwlicious[…]") → 9–10s tight-zoom "Kiwli[glass]usly" hold (full card visible) → 11s crossfade back toward grape ("stical Kiwlici", muddy olive-purple blend) → 12–13s "Grap[glass]stical" hold again → 14s crossfade back to strawberry ("licious Grapt", loose strawberries/diamonds falling into frame) → 15–17s "Straw[glass]cious" medium-zoom, matching the 0–1s state and closing the loop.

## Reusable techniques
1. **Palindrome color-cycle loop** (A→B→C→B→A) — lets a short clip cover 3 product variants while still looping seamlessly, since the first and last visible states match exactly. Far cheaper to produce and to loop cleanly than a strict A→B→C→A cycle.
2. **Text-behind-splash compositing** — a product photo (splash/liquid/motion graphic) composited to occlude the middle letters of a giant wordmark, so oversized display type reads as "physically interacting with" the hero photo instead of sitting flat on top of it as a caption.
3. **Crash-zoom-while-chrome-resolves entrance** — open tight on the "money shot" (word + splash + product) with zero UI chrome visible, then push the camera in further while nav/chips/controls fade in around the now-tighter crop — chrome earns its place on screen only after the product has already been seen.
4. **Two-tier color depth per theme** — a lighter, heavily blurred ambient wash filling the whole canvas, plus a deeper-saturated version of the same hue inside the card itself. One palette, two intensities, reinforces "the card is a window" without adding a second color.
5. **Theme-tinted type fill** — the hero wordmark's "white" is never neutral; it's warmed/cooled a few percent toward each active theme color (cream-pink for red, cream-lavender for purple, cream-yellow-green for kiwi). Cheap, subtle, and it keeps supposedly-white type from ever looking like a static sticker slapped over three different backgrounds.
6. **Adjective/pun compound wordmarks per SKU** ("Strawlicious," "Graptastical," "Kiwliciously") — turns the product name itself into the hero headline, eliminating the need for separate "headline + product name" copy blocks.
7. **Floating decorative particles tied to the product's real ingredients** (whole/halved fruit pieces, not abstract shapes) drifting at independent phases/speeds — reinforces "fresh, alive, in motion" without needing a literal video background everywhere.
8. **Quiet adjective chip row above the hero word** ("Fresh / Sweet / Irresistible") — thin unfilled pill outlines in a soft supporting weight, giving the hero a "confirmed by the brand voice" feel without competing with the giant wordmark for attention.
9. **Compact side info-panel formula**: thumbnail photo + short heading + 2-line caption + primary CTA pill + secondary circular play/video button, all in one small horizontal cluster — a reusable "product card in miniature" that can sit inside any hero without needing its own section.
10. **Single rounded "glass card" as the entire hero container** (same technique as T29) — nav, wordmark, photo, chips, panel and carousel controls all nested inside one bordered/radius'd window floating on an ambient backdrop, so the whole hero reads as "a screen sitting on the page."

## Autari adaptation
Dark Ink `#0B0E12` background, Pine `#0E7C66` → Spark `#19D3A2` green glow as the primary accent, optional Violet `#7C5CFF` as a rare secondary nod. Positioning: **"AI employees across ANY field, sold via consultation."** CTA: **"Book a call"** → `noor@autari.co.uk`.

Specific zone-by-zone swaps:
- **Color-theme cycle → field cycle**: replace the Strawberry/Grape/Kiwi palindrome cycle (A→B→C→B→A) with a **Finance → Support → Operations → Support → Finance** cycle — same palindrome structure so the loop still closes cleanly. Each "flavor" becomes a field/vertical: re-tint the ambient wash + card interior + wordmark fill per field using restrained variations on our own system (e.g., Pine/Spark green for the "home" state, a cooler blue-green or the Violet accent for a second field, a warmer amber-green for a third) rather than three unrelated hues — unlike the juice brand, our fields shouldn't each get a wildly different color family; keep everything inside the Ink/Pine/Spark/Violet system so the cycle reads as "one product, flexible surface" rather than "three different brands."
- **Giant wordmark → role/field name**: replace "Strawlicious / Graptastical / Kiwliciously" with our own compound role-headline per field, e.g. **"FinanceReady," "SupportAlways," "OpsRelentless"** (or simpler: just the field name set at the same giant scale — "Finance," "Support," "Operations" — if a punning wordmark reads too playful for our positioning). Keep the exact text-behind-splash technique, but swap the splash-glass hero photo for our existing liquid-black-chrome/crystal-glass hero form (from `hero_v1`/`hero.mp4`) with its pulsing emerald core sitting in the same "physically occluding the middle letters" position.
- **Fresh / Sweet / Irresistible chip row → outcome chips**: keep the exact thin-outline, sentence-case, quiet-weight pill formula, but swap the copy to short outcome adjectives that support the field, e.g. **"Fast," "Accurate," "Tireless"** — same restrained supporting-voice role above the giant wordmark, never competing with it.
- **Side info-panel → the 3 metric cards**: repurpose the thumbnail+heading+caption+CTA+play-button cluster as our **"40+ hrs/mo saved / 24-7 always-on / 1 human review"** trio. Concretely: keep the two small stacked glass icon tiles (checkmark micro-bullets) as quick trust markers ("SOC2-minded," "Human-reviewed," or similar), keep the product-thumbnail slot but replace it with a small looping clip of the Autari hero form instead of a smoothie glass, and swap the pill CTA + play-button pair for **"Book a call"** (pill, mailto `noor@autari.co.uk`) sitting beside a small circular "▶ Watch a 60s demo" button — same shape, same physical relationship, new payload.
- **Nav dropdown pill (flavor selector) → field selector**: the top-right chevron dropdown is a near-perfect steal — repurpose it verbatim as our **field/vertical selector** ("Choose your field ▾"), driving the exact same color-cycle + wordmark-swap transition described above when the user picks Finance/Support/Operations instead of a flavor.
- **Carousel prev/next arrows**: keep verbatim as manual controls to step through fields/roles on demand, supplementing (not replacing) the automatic palindrome auto-cycle.
- **Floating fruit particles → floating "proof" particles**: replace the drifting strawberry/grape/kiwi pieces with small drifting UI-proof glyphs relevant to each field (a tiny invoice/checkmark icon for Finance, a chat-bubble icon for Support, a gear/workflow icon for Operations) — same independent-phase floating motion, tied to real product substance instead of literal fruit.
- **Ambient ink-glow backdrop**: replace the soft coral/mauve/olive blurred wash with a Dark-Ink-based glow — deep `#0B0E12` at the canvas edges softening into a Pine `#0E7C66`-to-Spark `#19D3A2` bloom immediately around the card (and a Violet `#7C5CFF` bloom variant for whichever field state uses the secondary accent) — same two-tier depth technique (light blurred wash outside, deeper saturated tone inside the card) but built from our own three-color system instead of three unrelated fruit hues.
- **What NOT to copy**: skip the illegible micro-nav-wordmark and micro-dropdown-label problem this template has — everything in our nav must stay legible at normal viewing size (a lesson this very transcription effort proves the hard way: several of T9's own nav elements are sub-pixel and unreadable even in the source asset). Also don't copy the "adverb-form" inconsistency in the kiwi wordmark ("Kiwliciously" vs. the adjective-form siblings) — keep our per-field headline grammar consistent across every cycle state.
