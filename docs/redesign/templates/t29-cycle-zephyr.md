## Brand & story
Fictional brand **"CYCLE"** (wordmark in the nav, icon = a stacked double-chevron/paper-plane glyph — two solid triangular arrow shapes nested pointing right, like a ">>" fast-forward mark rendered as one glyph). The product is a single premium road bike named **"Zephyr"**, presented as item **"09"** in a numbered catalog under the sub-brand/model-line tag **"VYPER"** (eyebrow reads "09 / VYPER"). Marketing copy frames it as a performance/lifestyle object ("the revolutionary Zephyr bicycle"), not a utility bike — the tone is boutique-automotive: one hero object, shot like a studio product photo, lit almost entirely in shadow so the carbon-fiber form reads through reflection and rim-light rather than color or texture detail. The story being sold is precision-engineering + exclusivity: a catalog of 5 items (numbered index 01–05) of which this is the one on display, with spec-sheet-style stats (range, charge time, a voltage-style gauge reading) presented like a vehicle dashboard rather than plain product bullets.

## Layout — zone by zone
The entire hero lives inside **one large rounded-corner card** (thin ~1–2px bright white/off-white stroke, large corner radius, near-black `#0D0D0D`–`#141414` interior) that itself sits on a solid black page background. Reading the card top-to-bottom, left-to-right:

- **Top nav bar** (inside the card, top edge, sitting on a very slightly lighter dark strip than the card body): left — the double-chevron "CYCLE" wordmark/logo. Center-right — a small magnifying-glass search icon. Right of that — three uppercase, letter-spaced, light-weight nav links in a single row: **"COLLECTION"**, **"DESIGNS"**, **"PATTERNS"**. Far right — a black filled pill button: a small white circle containing a black triangular navigation/location-arrow glyph (pointed up-left, like a compass needle or "send" icon), followed by the uppercase label **"CONTACT"**.
- **Hero bike, center**: the Zephyr bike shot in tight 3/4-front framing so the drop handlebars, stem, and front wheel/fork dominate the foreground (matte-black carbon frame, visible dark decal on the down tube, disc brake, thin spokes), with the rear wheel and saddle receding into deeper shadow toward the right/top of the frame. The bike is lit almost entirely by rim-light/reflection — no fill light — so the silhouette reads more than the surface detail (true "black-on-black" product photography).
- **Faded oversized product behind**: a second, much larger and darker/desaturated duplicate of a bike (different crop — top of the page shows a seat/seatpost/top-tube, bottom of the page below the card shows a crank arm/pedal/chainring) bleeds off the top and bottom edges of the visible canvas, behind and around the card. It reads as ambient environment/depth rather than a second product — barely-there luminance shifts on pure black, no distinct color.
- **Dark glass stat chips** (upper-right/mid-right of the card, cascading diagonally downward, each chip overlapping the one above-left of it slightly): chip 1 — **"32KM"** (bold white "32", lighter-weight grey "KM") with a smaller grey caption **"Range"** beneath; chip 2, positioned lower and further right, overlapping chip 1's bottom-right corner — **"3.2H"** (bold white "3.2", lighter "H") with smaller grey caption **"Charge Time"** beneath. Chips are frosted/glass dark rounded-squares (`~#101010`–`#161616`), no visible border beyond a subtle lighter glass edge.
- **Circular gauge dial** (below the two chips, lower-right of the card): a round graphite-grey dial (`~#202226`–`#232428`) with tick marks around the top arc like a speedometer/voltmeter face, a small dark engraved icon at its center (abstract — reads as a crossed needle/compass or wrench-like mark, not fully legible), and a label above the dial reading **"12.8kV"** (bold "12.8", lighter "kV"). At the very base of the dial face there is one nearly-imperceptible warm/maroon micro-highlight — the single non-neutral pixel cluster found anywhere in the frame; it's easy to miss and reads as a rendered shadow/needle-tip highlight rather than a deliberate brand color, but it is the one departure from pure black/white/grey in the entire composition.
- **Numbered vertical index** (far left edge of the card, outside the bike photo, next to a thin vertical accent line running top-to-bottom): **01** (bold, full-white, "active" state), **02**, **03**, **04**, **05** (all grey/ghosted, "inactive"), stacked with even vertical spacing — implies a 5-item slider/gallery even though only one state is shown.
- **Headline block** (bottom-left of the card, overlapping the bottom edge of the bike photo): eyebrow tag **"09 / VYPER"** in thin, heavily letter-spaced uppercase, immediately followed by a thin horizontal rule line extending right. Below it, the huge bold condensed all-caps headline **"ZEPHYR"** in pure white. To the right of the headline, small grey body copy in two lines: **"Experience the future of cycling with the revolutionary Zephyr bicycle today."**
- **Inset thumbnail card** (bottom-left corner, partly overlapping/nested into the headline block and the card's own rounded corner): a smaller secondary product photo (close crop of the bike's front wheel/fork) inside its own rounded card whose top-right corner is cut away on a diagonal ("dog-ear"/torn-corner shape), with a small **↗ diagonal arrow glyph** sitting in that cut-away notch — a "view more / expand" affordance.
- **Scroll cue**: not visible as a distinct separate control in the resting frame (no explicit "SCROLL" badge like sibling templates T21/T28/T37) — the numbered index and the single-viewport loop imply the interaction model is "cycle through 01–05" rather than "scroll down."

## Palette
Near-total monochrome — the palette discipline itself is the design statement.
- `#000000` (pure black) — outer page background and the deepest shadow areas of the bike.
- `#0D0D0D`–`#141414` (near-black, very subtle warmth) — card interior background, bike frame midtones.
- `#1E1E1E`–`#222222` (dark charcoal) — nav bar strip, slightly lifted off the card background to separate it visually.
- `#101010`–`#161616` (glass dark) — stat-chip fills, just barely distinguishable from the card background via a faint lighter glass edge.
- `#202226`–`#232428` (graphite grey) — the circular gauge dial face, the one element with genuine mid-grey value rather than nearly-black.
- `#F5F5F5`–`#FFFFFF` (true white / off-white) — all headline type, chip numerals, nav wordmark, card border stroke, numbered-index "01" active state.
- `~#8A8A8A`–`#9A9A9A` (mid grey) — secondary/caption text: nav links, chip unit letters and captions, body copy, inactive index numbers (02–05), eyebrow tag.
- A single near-invisible warm/maroon micro-highlight (~`#5A1F1F`–`#7A2A2A` range, unconfirmed exact value — sub-pixel scale) at the base of the gauge dial — the only non-neutral color note in the whole frame.

## Typography
- **Display/headline ("ZEPHYR")**: bold, condensed, tightly-kerned grotesque sans-serif, all-caps, set very large relative to the card — the single loudest visual element after the bike itself.
- **Eyebrow ("09 / VYPER")**: same or a lighter-weight cut of the same family, small size, heavy positive letter-spacing (tracking wide enough that each character/digit reads almost individually), all-caps, paired with a thin horizontal rule.
- **Nav links / chip captions / body copy**: a lighter-weight cut of the same grotesque family (or a close geometric-sans companion), small size, moderate letter-spacing on the all-caps nav items, normal-case sentence spacing on the body copy.
- **Stat numerals ("32KM", "3.2H", "12.8kV")**: the number itself set bold/heavy, the unit suffix immediately after it set in a visibly lighter weight and often slightly smaller — a two-weight micro-contrast used consistently across every stat readout in the design.
- Overall type voice: engineered, condensed, technical — no serif, no script, no soft rounded humanist forms anywhere. Reinforces the "spec sheet / dashboard" reading of the whole hero.

## Motion — every element
Reconstructed from comparing the GIF's captured moment (a tight, chrome-free crop on just the handlebars, rounded card border visible, no nav/chips/gauge/headline yet rendered) against the full resting-state frame (every UI layer present) — this is a **tight-crop-to-full-reveal entrance loop**, not a scroll-driven multi-scene demo:

- **Entrance (card scales in)**: the sequence opens tight and cropped — the rounded card is already present (its white stroke visible) but framed close on the handlebar/stem detail only, on near-total black, with no nav bar, no stat chips, no gauge, no headline, no index yet on screen. From there the framing pulls back / the card's content resolves outward, revealing the full bike (both wheels, saddle, frame) inside the same card boundary. This reads as either a camera dolly-out on the product shot or a scale/crop-in animation on the image itself, timed to the card's own entrance.
- **Staggered chrome reveal**: as the full bike resolves, the surrounding UI chrome fades/slides into place in a staggered sequence rather than all at once — nav bar (logo, links, Contact pill) settles at the top; the two stat chips fade or slide in from the right, chip 1 ("32KM") first, chip 2 ("3.2H") a beat after, cascading into their overlapping stacked position; the gauge dial fades in below them with its needle sweeping from a rest position up to the "12.8kV" reading (a classic dashboard needle-sweep, matching the speedometer-tick styling of the dial face); the numbered vertical index (01–05) fades in on the left edge with "01" already in its bright/active state; the headline block (eyebrow → rule line → "ZEPHYR" → body copy) rises/fades in last at the bottom, with the inset thumbnail card sliding in from the bottom-left corner to nest into the headline block's corner.
- **Ambient / idle loop**: once fully resolved, the scene holds in a subtle "never fully still" idle state — expected micro-motion consistent with this template family: a faint continuous parallax drift on the oversized faded bike bleeding behind the card (slow, sub-pixel vertical drift, reinforcing depth without ever fully entering/exiting frame), and a very slight breathing/shimmer on the gauge needle or dial ticks to keep the "live dashboard" read alive rather than a frozen screenshot.
- **Transitions**: the whole loop plays as one continuous forward motion (tight crop → full reveal → held state) then presumably resets/loops back to the tight crop rather than cross-fading to a second distinct scene — there is no evidence of a second headline, second product angle, or color-theme change anywhere in the captured frames, unlike T9/T27/T38's multi-state cycles.
- **Scroll / parallax (faded background product)**: the oversized, faded duplicate bike bleeding above and below the card is the template's parallax layer — on a real implementation this would drift at a different speed than the card during scroll (classic background-slower-than-foreground parallax), reinforcing that the card is a "window" floating in front of a much larger, darker version of the same product.
- **Per-element motion**:
  - Card: scale/crop entrance (tight → full), then static rounded "window" frame.
  - Bike (foreground): reveals via the crop pull-back; effectively static afterward (no visible rotation loop captured, unlike T22/T28's rotating hero objects — this hero product is presented more like a locked studio photograph than a living 3D render).
  - Nav bar: fades/settles in at top, static afterward.
  - Stat chips: staggered fade/slide-in from the right, holding in their cascaded/overlapping position.
  - Gauge dial: fades in, needle sweeps from rest to "12.8kV," subtle idle tick/shimmer afterward.
  - Numbered index: fades in with "01" pre-lit; on a live site this would presumably re-light 02→05 on interaction/scroll (the ghosted-but-legible 02–05 numerals strongly imply an interactive slider state even though only "01" is captured active here).
  - Headline block: eyebrow + rule + "ZEPHYR" + body copy rise/fade in as one grouped block, last to arrive.
  - Inset thumbnail: slides in from bottom-left last, nesting its cut corner + arrow into the headline block's corner.
  - Background oversized bike: continuous slow ambient parallax drift, present throughout, never resolving into a "real" separate section.

## Reusable techniques
1. **Near-monochrome black-on-black identity** — every asset, background, and UI chrome kept within one hue family (black/near-black/charcoal/grey/white); the only relief is a razor-thin bright card stroke and pure-white type. Produces an extremely premium, editorial, "everything is one material" feel without needing a second color.
2. **Oversized faded product duplicate bleeding behind the framed card** — the same product, shot larger and darker, dropped in exposure, extending past the card's own edges top and bottom. Implies scale and depth using only luminance, no second asset design needed, and reads as ambient environment rather than clutter.
3. **Rounded "window" card as the entire hero container** — a single thin-stroke, large-radius rounded rectangle holds nav + product + copy + chips all inside one bordered frame, so the whole hero reads as "a screen sitting on the page" (a physical bezel/device metaphor) rather than a flat section.
4. **Dark glass stat chips cascading diagonally** — 2+ chips staggered from upper area down to mid-area, each slightly overlapping the previous, each following a "big bold number + small lighter unit + tiny grey label" formula. Overlap communicates a physical stack of small dashboard readouts rather than a flat list.
5. **Circular analog gauge as a stat, not another chip** — one metric rendered as a real instrument face (speedometer ticks + needle + unit label above) instead of a third rectangular chip. Breaks chip-repetition monotony and reinforces an "engineered machine, not a webpage" story.
6. **Vertical numbered index (01–05) with one lit / rest ghosted** — implies a multi-item slider/gallery even in a single static hero shot; costs almost nothing visually (five small numerals + one accent line) but strongly signals "there is more, this is item one of five."
7. **Corner-clipped inset thumbnail with a "view/expand" arrow glyph** — a secondary photo nested into a main card's own corner, its top-right corner diagonally cut away ("dog-ear") to hold a small arrow icon — a compact, decorative way to hint "tap for more views" without a separate button.
8. **Tight-crop-to-full-reveal entrance** — open the whole sequence on an extreme, chrome-free close-up of one product detail (here: handlebars only, no UI at all visible), then pull back / resolve so the entire hero UI (nav, chips, gauge, copy, index) fades and scales into place around the now-fully-framed product. Teases craft/material before the "sales pitch" chrome ever appears.
9. **Eyebrow catalog-index tag + rule line** ("09 / VYPER") — a tiny, heavily-tracked "you are looking at item 09 of a numbered collection" tag sitting directly above the headline, paired with a thin horizontal rule. Reinforces a curated-catalog narrative even when only one hero item is ever shown.
10. **Single filled icon+label pill as the nav's terminal CTA** ("Contact", with a location/compass-arrow icon in a white circle) — the nav ends in exactly one black pill rather than a row of buttons, keeping the whole nav reading as minimal/technical rather than commerce-heavy.

## Autari adaptation
Keep the discipline, not the bike. This template's entire value for Autari is that it proves **near-monochrome ink + exactly one accent** reads as premium and expensive rather than plain — which is precisely our stated palette rule (Dark Ink `#0B0E12` background, Pine `#0E7C66` → Spark `#19D3A2` as the single primary glow, optional Violet `#7C5CFF` as a rare secondary nod). Where T29 spends its one accent on a barely-there maroon gauge micro-highlight, we spend ours deliberately and visibly on the Spark green glow — same restraint, opposite confidence: our accent should be seen, not almost-hidden.

Specific zone-by-zone swaps:
- **Nav**: replace the double-chevron "CYCLE" mark with an Autari spark/asterisk glyph + wordmark. Replace "COLLECTION / DESIGNS / PATTERNS" with our own top-level links (e.g. "How it works / Roles / Pricing"). Keep the exact **single filled pill nav-cap pattern**, but swap "Contact" (location-arrow icon + label) for **"Book a call"** (icon: a small calendar/arrow-send glyph in a white or Spark-green-lit circle) → `mailto:noor@autari.co.uk`. This is a direct, near-zero-effort steal: same shape, same position, same "one pill ends the nav" restraint.
- **Hero subject**: replace the matte-black bike with our existing liquid-black-chrome + crystal-glass hero form with the pulsing emerald core (`hero_v1` / `hero.mp4`). Keep the same tight-crop-to-full-reveal entrance — open on an extreme close-up of the glass/chrome surface (no chrome UI visible), then pull back to reveal the full hero form with nav/chips/gauge/copy resolving in around it, exactly matching T29's staggered reveal choreography.
- **Faded oversized background duplicate**: keep this technique verbatim — a much larger, darker, desaturated duplicate of the hero form bleeding above/below the card, drifting slowly (parallax) — but grade it in Pine/Ink tones instead of pure grey, so the "shadow twin" still reads as green-black rather than neutral-black.
- **Stat chips** (32km Range / 3.2h Charge → ours): "40+ hrs/mo saved" and "24/7 always-on," same cascading-overlap two-chip formation, same big-bold-number + small-lighter-unit + tiny-grey-caption formula, same dark-glass fill — just recolor the glass border/glow with a hairline Spark-green edge instead of neutral grey.
- **Circular gauge dial** (12.8kV → ours): this is the single best steal in the whole template — repurpose it as a live-feeling trust metric, e.g. **"1 human review"** (needle pointing to a "low-touch" zone on the dial) or an "active now" percentage dial showing AI-employee uptime. Keep the analog speedometer-tick face and needle-sweep entrance animation; light the needle tip in Spark green instead of the near-invisible maroon T29 uses — ours should glow, not hide.
- **Numbered vertical index** (01–05 → ours): repurpose as the How-it-works steps, matching the existing template-study mapping — `[001] Tell us the role · [002] We build it to your firm · [003] It starts working` — rendered as a lit-vs-ghosted vertical list exactly like T29's 01/02/03/04/05, with the active step lit in white/Spark-green and the rest ghosted grey.
- **Inset corner thumbnail + arrow**: repurpose as a "See a real day-in-the-life" nested thumbnail/link nested in the hero card's corner, same dog-ear cut + ↗ arrow, linking to the scroll/day-in-the-life section instead of another product photo.
- **Eyebrow tag** ("09 / VYPER" → ours): a small catalog-style tag above the headline naming the currently-featured AI-employee role, e.g. "00X / [ROLE NAME]" with the same heavy tracking + rule line, reinforcing "AI employees across ANY field" — swap the role name per vertical/persona shown.
- **Headline + body copy**: "ZEPHYR" → our own bold condensed headline (Space Grotesk display per the system type direction); body copy swapped to our positioning line, e.g. "AI employees that run repetitive work — sold to you through a real consultation, not a signup form."
- **Typography**: match T29's two-weight numeral trick (bold number + light unit) on every one of our stat readouts — it's cheap to implement and reads instantly as "engineered/precise," which is exactly the trust signal Autari needs against "just another SaaS dashboard."
- **What NOT to copy**: T29's near-invisible single color accent is a weakness in isolation — for Autari the green glow must be confident and legible (per the Ink/Pine/Spark system), not buried as a barely-there pixel. Also skip T29's "static hero product" choice (no rotation loop) — our hero form should keep its existing pulsing/rotating ambient motion since "living, always-on" is core to the Autari story, unlike T29's locked studio-photo bike.
