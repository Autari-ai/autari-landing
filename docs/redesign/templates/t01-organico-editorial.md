# t01 — hero-organico-editorial
**Source:** https://figma-templates-en.vercel.app/assets/template%201/hero-organico-editorial.webm
**Duration:** ~27.7s
**Orientation:** Landscape — a wide rounded-rectangle "browser card" (roughly 16:9) floats inside a continuous 3D diorama world. The clip opens with a device-mockup (laptop) framing device, then the camera pushes in through the laptop screen into the card itself and never shows a device frame again.

## Brand & story
This is not a single static hero — it is a mini anthology trailer for a landing-page template, structured as four self-contained "chapters," each its own headline + scene + color world, stitched together with in-camera zoom-through-portal transitions (the camera dives into a small nested card/vortex inside the current scene and that nested content becomes the next full-frame scene). The throughline is a whimsical, pastel, claymation-style "organic" 3D world (rolling grass hills, pastel skies, floating candy-colored spheres) that persists as the outer environment behind/around the browser card in chapters 1–2, then the world itself darkens and shifts into a swirling dream-portal for chapter 3, then resolves into a realistic cloudscape for chapter 4. Each chapter demonstrates a different hero content pattern (headline+CTA, headline+social-proof chips, headline+character illustration, headline+cycling stat badges) — effectively a showreel of hero variants rather than one fixed layout. Tone: playful, editorial, dreamlike, storybook/"organic" (hence the template name "hero-organico-editorial").

## Typography
- **Eyebrow/micro-label:** small caps, a glitch/pixel/dot-matrix display face with a scanline-and-chromatic-aberration treatment — deliberately "broken digital" feeling, used consistently as the smaller line above every big headline word across all four chapters.
- **Display headline:** large, heavy-weight, rounded-geometric sans, all caps, solid white, no italic, tight letter-spacing, 1–3 lines depending on chapter length ("JOURNEY"; "DANCING THROUGH ✦ UNIVERSES"; "MEET / THE LITTLE / DREAMER"; "BEYOND THE / CLOUDY HORIZON"). No serif anywhere.
- **Body copy:** small, regular-weight, white/light-gray sans, 2–5 lines, left-aligned under the headline, low-contrast/secondary role — content is illegible at source resolution but structurally consistent across chapters (short descriptive paragraph beneath every headline).
- **Chip/badge labels:** small bold caps (e.g. "BLOOM"), paired with a lighter-weight numeric/percent value beside it.

## Motion — every element
- **Entrance:** Cold open on a laptop-mockup (0s) with the hero already visible in miniature on its screen; camera immediately dollies/zooms into the laptop screen (0s→1s) so the screen content fills frame and the device disappears entirely by 1s.
- **Ambient / idle loop (continuous throughout):** the outer 3D world never stops — pastel spheres drift and bob slowly across the hills, tiny white sparkle particles twinkle across the grass, clouds drift laterally, and the whole hill terrain has a very slow parallax creep (as if a static camera is very slowly craning/panning across the diorama) underneath whatever chapter content is showing in the card.
- **Transitions between scenes:** every chapter change is an in-camera zoom-through-portal — never a hard cut or slide. A small nested card/orb (the "portal") appears mid-scene, the camera pushes into it, and its contents expand to fill the whole frame as the next chapter. Concretely: Ch.1→Ch.2 via a small rounded photo-card that scales up (4s–7s); Ch.2→Ch.3 via a literal spiral/vortex the whole scene curls into (11s–14s); Ch.3→Ch.4 via the vortex collapsing to a white flash/light-burst that resolves into clouds (18s–20s).
- **Scroll / parallax / 3D tilt / scrub:** no explicit scroll-jacking is shown (this is an autoplaying loop/trailer, not a scroll-driven demo), but there is continuous subtle parallax between the browser-card (foreground, fixed) and the outer diorama (background, slow drift) — reads as a lightweight 3D-tilt/parallax layer rather than a literal scroll interaction.
- **Per-element motion:**
  - *Headline text:* fades/glitches in with the eyebrow line rendering first in a stuttering, flickering "digital glitch" reveal (scanline flicker + chromatic-fringe jitter) before the bold display word settles in clean and static — a text-reveal effect rather than text-behind-subject.
  - *Floating spheres:* slow independent bob/orbit motion, some rolling very slightly down the hill slopes, continuous and non-synchronized (organic, not mechanical).
  - *Sun/cloud icon (Ch.1 portal card):* the miniature sun icon has a subtle idle bounce/glow pulse while its containing card scales up during the transition.
  - *Social-proof avatar chips (Ch.2):* appear with a slight staggered pop-in (one appears just after the other), otherwise static once settled.
  - *Character mascot (Ch.3):* floats with a gentle continuous bob/sway (arms out, flying pose), hair/clothing has subtle secondary motion; sits inside a diamond card that itself may rotate very slightly.
  - *Portal/vortex (Ch.2→3 and background of Ch.3):* continuously spins/swirls, color churns from maroon through teal/blue/purple, never fully static — used both as a transition device and as ambient background texture once in Ch.3.
  - *Stat/spec chips (Ch.4):* hard-cut/cross-fade swap every ~1 second cycling through at least 3 icon+label variants (checklist, map-pin, warning-triangle), each held centered over the headline with no drag/parallax of its own — a simple sequential showcase rather than a physically-simulated stack.
  - *CTA pill button:* static shape, no idle animation observed beyond being part of the overall card; likely has a hover/press state not visible in an autoplaying trailer.

## Reusable techniques
- Two-tier nav (simulated browser chrome strip stacked directly above the real site nav) instantly reads as "this is a live website," reinforcing that the template is real and functional, not just a poster.
- Zoom-through-portal scene transitions: hide the cut by pre-placing a smaller "next scene" object in-frame (a card, a vortex, a light burst) and simply pushing the camera into it — makes multi-chapter hero reels feel like one continuous shot instead of a slideshow.
- Two-font headline pairing: a "broken/glitch" display face for the short eyebrow line contrasted with a clean heavy geometric sans for the actual headline word — cheap way to add editorial personality without sacrificing legibility on the words that matter most.
- Letting the environment BE the hero visual: no product screenshot or photograph is needed when the background itself (terrain + floating objects) carries the brand's visual identity; content chapters are just headline+CTA+chips laid over that same living backdrop.
- Chapter-tinted chrome: re-skinning the nav bar's background tint to match each scene's dominant color (rather than keeping a fixed neutral bar) makes the nav feel integrated into each mini-story instead of a static overlay.
- Cycling stat/spec chip carousel: a single fixed-position card that swaps icon+label+value every ~1s is a very cheap way to communicate "many features/benefits" in a few seconds without adding new layout complexity.
- Outer-world continuity: keeping the surrounding diorama (hills, spheres, sky) visually constant across chapters — even while the *card content* changes completely — gives the whole piece a "same universe, different page" cohesion.
