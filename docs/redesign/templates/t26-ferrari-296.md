**Source / Duration / Orientation**

- Duration: ~28.3s (measured directly from the supplied preview GIF: 85 frames at 330ms/frame = 28.05s, matching the stated ~28.3s)
- Orientation: Desktop landscape, full-bleed browser-style webpage screenshot (no device-card tilt like some other templates in this set — the mockup sits flat, edge-to-edge). The mockup is presented centered against an **outer backdrop that is itself a heavily blurred, extended version of the same scene's dominant color** — i.e. the page "furniture" beyond the screenshot isn't neutral grey, it's a soft-focus bleed of whatever hue the hero is currently graded in. This outer bleed is a key piece of evidence for the monochrome-tint technique (see Palette/Reusable techniques below): the tint isn't just applied to the screenshot content, it's applied to the presentation frame around it too.

## Brand & story

A generic, white-label automotive showcase/configurator template wearing a "Ferrari 296 GTB" skin as its demo content — not a real Ferrari property. Two pieces of evidence prove this is stock/swappable content, not a bespoke Ferrari build: (1) a row of small circular brand-logo badges (Mercedes-Benz tri-star, a Lexus "L," a BMW roundel, alongside a Ferrari-shield-style glyph) appears bottom-left during the mid-sequence chapter, functioning as a "works across marques" strip; (2) the floating spec card's numbers ("15h Battery Life," "300mi Range," "120mph Speed") are placeholder demo stats that don't match a real 296 GTB's actual plug-in-hybrid figures — they exist to show the card component, not to be factually accurate.

Story arc: a cinematic "unveiling" — a single spotlight finds a cloth-draped car on a glossy dark stage, the cover is pulled back to reveal the hero object, and the reveal is immediately followed by a full-frame color-flash that recolors the entire world in one saturated brand hue. From there the hero plays as a **rotating carousel of "chapters,"** each pairing one nav label (Performance / Technology / Interior / Experience) with its own headline, camera angle, and supporting UI, all wrapped around the same 3D car asset. Two chapters were directly captured and are documented below: Chapter A, "VELOCITY BEYOND LIMITS" (brand-promise / hero-stat framing), and Chapter B, "CARBON FIBER CHASSIS" (engineering/spec framing — this is the frame supplied as the static reference `t26-a.jpeg`). Tone: moody, cinematic, performance-luxury — selling the fantasy of speed and craftsmanship, not a spec sheet, even though a spec-styled UI chip is present as set dressing.

## Palette

The entire piece is a **single rose/magenta hue family carried from near-black through to fully saturated hot pink** — it is not really a hue change between "chapters," it is one fixed hue ramped up and down in brightness/saturation. Sampled values:

| Hex | Role |
|---|---|
| `#07070A` – `#0A0A0D` | Near-black stage background, dark-reveal / cold-open state |
| `#7B2235` | Deep burgundy-red car paint base tone during the dark/reveal and Chapter A states — already magenta-shifted, not a neutral red |
| `#C73E5B` / `#D56079` | Brighter magenta-red specular highlights on the paint in the dark/reveal + Chapter A states (the "proto-pink" that later blooms into the full hero pink) |
| `#DCD8D8` / `#E1D9DB` | Cool white-grey spotlight beam / glass and chrome reflections |
| `#CE4560` → `#EC708C` | Primary background vertical gradient in Chapter B — deeper rose at the top (near the nav), lightening to a softer pink toward the floor |
| `#FD6180` | Brightest magenta-pink specular highlight on the car paint in the fully-tinted hero state |
| `#DF5871` | Feature-card pink base tone (Adaptive Suspension / Signature Lighting card backgrounds) |
| `#9F334E` | Dark maroon gradient/vignette overlay used to keep white card titles legible (e.g. under "Signature Lighting") |
| `#FFFFFF` / `#FEFEFE` | CTA pill backgrounds, "Digital Performance" card background, headline + nav text |
| near-black (~`#141414`) | CTA button text, "Digital Performance" card title/body text |

Overall palette logic: value and saturation do all the work — the same rose-magenta hue is dialed all the way down to near-black for drama in the reveal beat, then dialed all the way up to a hot, flat, almost neon pink for the confident "hero" beat. Nothing in the frame (car, floor, sky/backdrop, photography, decorative textures) is ever rendered in a competing hue; white is reserved exclusively for text/UI so it reads as "interface" against the "world."

## Typography

- **Headline**: bold, uppercase, tight-leading, geometric/grotesque sans display face, always exactly 3 stacked short lines, always left-aligned. Chapter B ("CARBON FIBER CHASSIS") uses one plain weight throughout — a blunt, structural, all-caps block. Chapter A ("VELOCITY / BEYOND / LIMITS") instead mixes an upright weight for the outer two lines with an italic slant on the middle line ("BEYOND") — the same "plain — emphasis word — plain" mixed-weight/italic device seen elsewhere in this template family, here used only for the more emotive/brand-promise chapter and dropped entirely for the more technical/spec chapter.
- **Body copy**: small, sentence case, regular/light weight, white or near-white, wrapped to 3–4 short lines — clearly secondary to the headline, same short single-sentence-of-value-prop pattern in both chapters.
- **Nav links**: small, title case (not full caps), light/white, thin weight, generously letter-spaced, low visual weight so they recede under the headline.
- **CTA label**: bold, uppercase, tracked-out, black-on-white — "DISCOVER VELOCITY" / "EXPLORE MORE."
- **Feature-card titles** ("Adaptive Suspension," "Digital Performance," "Signature Lighting"): a rounded, soft-geometric sans — visibly rounder terminals than the sharp headline face, two lines each (natural word-wrap), medium/semibold weight — functions as this template's distinct "component/UI" typeface, paired with a smaller regular-weight body line beneath each title in the same face.
- **Spec-card numerals** ("15h," "300mi," "120mph"): large bold figures paired with a small caption line underneath ("BATTERY LIFE," "RANGE," "SPEED") — the same big-number-plus-small-caption stat-chip pattern used across this template family for trust/metric callouts.

## Motion — every element

- **Entrance**:
  1. Cold open on a near-black studio stage; a hard-edged single spotlight beam cuts down from the top of frame onto a cloth/tarp-draped car silhouette sitting on a glossy reflective floor that mirrors the beam as a soft glowing pool. A circular "play/watch" glyph (thin ring + solid white triangle) sits centered on the covered car — read as a video-teaser affordance baked into the hero itself.
  2. The Chapter-A headline types on progressively, left to right / line by line — captured mid-type reading "VELOC," then "VELOCITY / BEYO​ND / LI," before settling on the complete "VELOCITY / BEYOND / LIMITS."
  3. As the headline completes, the dust cover visibly animates off the car — the cloth lifts/slides away like a sheet being pulled back, exposing glossy paint and a sliver of lit headlight before the whole car is shown.
  4. In the same beat: the body-copy line fades in, the white "EXPLORE MORE →" pill fades/slides in beneath it, the small brand-logo badge row fades in bottom-left, and the floating spec card animates in bottom-right — its three stat numerals visibly **count up from 0** to their resting values ("15h," "300mi," "120mph") rather than simply appearing; this is corroborated by unusually large frame-to-frame pixel deltas measured specifically across this window (well above the idle baseline — see Transitions below).
- **Ambient / idle loop** (within any held chapter): the car performs a slow continuous rotation/orbit and/or gentle vertical float, sweeping its specular highlights across the paint; the floor reflection shimmers faintly in sync; the background gradient holds essentially static aside from a very soft breathing brightness around the light source. Text, CTA, cards, and nav are static once settled — no idle motion on UI once it has entered.
- **Transitions**: the signature move is a **full-frame color flash-wipe** — for one to two frames the entire composition (car, floor, backdrop, and the blurred outer page bleed) floods to a single flat, fully saturated magenta-pink, then the next chapter fades up already tinted in that color. This was measured directly: ordinary idle-loop motion produces a frame-to-frame mean pixel delta in the low single digits to ~20; the flash-wipe moments spike to 85–116 — by far the largest deltas in the entire clip — confirming these are hard color-flood beats, not gradual cross-dissolves. The car's camera angle also changes across each wipe (front-3/4 before, rear-3/4 immediately after), so the flash reads as a full turntable spin happening "inside" the color flood.
- **Scroll / parallax**: no literal scroll-jacked 3D card tilt. The "scroll" cue is structural/implicit — the 4-item nav (Performance/Technology/Interior/Experience) stands in for a progress index, and the carousel auto-advances chapter to chapter on its own timer rather than being driven by user scroll. Within a held chapter, a very slow camera push-in/drift on the car+floor+background gives a mild sense of depth while the foreground UI (headline, CTA, cards) stays perfectly pinned/static — a light parallax cue rather than the dominant technique.
- **Per-element motion (feature-card reveal, Chapter B)**: the right-side 2×3 grid reveals **top-to-bottom, staggered, one row at a time** — each card is swept by a thin bright horizontal scan-line/light-bar, with the card's title/photo content appearing to materialize behind the sweeping line rather than simply fading in. A captured mid-reveal frame shows "Adaptive Suspension" already fully settled while "Signature Lighting" is still mid-sweep beneath its own bar lower in the stack, confirming the sequential (not simultaneous) top-down reveal order; each row's photo card resolves in the same beat as its paired text card. The two bottom-left inset thumbnails also stagger in individually (one frame shows only the left thumbnail present, joined by the second a beat later) rather than appearing together. The Chapter-B headline itself also types/wipes on the same way Chapter A's does (a mid-transition frame catches it reading "ARBON" / "FIB," still resolving, mid-pan).

## Reusable techniques

- **One color-grade, every layer.** The tint isn't matched shot-by-shot; a single rose-magenta LUT is applied across the hero object, the environment, the supporting photography, decorative textures, AND the blurred outer page backdrop, so a dozen visually unrelated source assets (3D car render, portrait photography, interior photography, abstract texture overlays) all read as one continuous film.
- **Flash-wipe as scene transition.** A full-frame flood to a single flat brand color (1–2 frames) between chapters is cheaper to produce and reads more "branded" than a cross-dissolve — the transition itself becomes a piece of brand expression instead of a neutral cut.
- **Cloth-cover unveiling as a universal "reveal" opener** — spotlit stage, draped hero object, cover animates off — a portable metaphor for any hero object, physical or abstract.
- **Type-on headline** synced to the product-reveal beat rather than a plain fade/slide adds a "being written live" feel that ties copy and visual reveal together.
- **Counting-up stat chip** (thumbnail + label + N stat tiles whose numerals animate from 0 to their resting value) is a compact, reusable trust/spec callout.
- **Small circular partner/brand-logo row** as a lightweight, low-effort "compatible with / trusted across" signal.
- **Checkerboard text-card/photo-card grid** (2 columns × N rows, text and photo swapping sides each row) turns a plain feature list into a scannable, visually rich stack.
- **Scan-line/light-bar sweep as a card-reveal mechanic** — feels more "high-tech" than an opacity fade and is trivial to stagger for sequential multi-card reveals.
- **Nav-label-as-carousel-index** — tying each top-nav item to its own hero chapter turns ordinary nav copy into an implicit progress/story index without needing visible dots or arrows.

## Autari adaptation

**The single most valuable steal from this template is the monochrome brand-tint-on-everything technique** — replicate it exactly, just swap the hue family from rose-magenta to Dark-Ink/Pine/Spark green. Concretely: generate every hero asset (the dark "reveal" stage render, the hero AI-core/object render, feature-card photography or product screenshots, ambient background plates, and any decorative texture overlays) in **neutral, roughly desaturated lighting first** — do not bake a color mood into any individual source asset. Then, in one shared compositing/export pass, apply a **single duotone map to every layer at once**, including the blurred outer page backdrop: shadows/dark values → Dark Ink `#0B0E12`, midtones → Pine `#0E7C66`, highlights/speculars → Spark `#19D3A2`. This is exactly how t26 makes a spotlight stage, a burgundy car, a hot-pink car, driving-portrait photography, and interior-detail photography all feel like stills from one film — it's one LUT over everything, not per-asset color matching. Optionally reserve Violet `#7C5CFF` the way t26 reserves its brightest magenta highlight — as a rare, secondary rim-light/accent (e.g., one feature-card accent, or a rim-light opposite the green core) rather than a competing primary.
