## Brand & story
Fictional brand: **"CHRYSANT"** (wordmark shown top-left nav, bold uppercase, letter-spaced small-caps sans-serif). The hero headline reads **"CRYSTAL LOTUS"** — a stylized/marketing name for what the body copy reveals is botanically a **chrysanthemum** ("Frost Dahlia" is the specific cultivar shown, and the secondary panel explicitly says "the chrysanthemum represents longevity, optimism, and resilience"). So the template's internal story: a luxury botanical/jewelry-adjacent brand that renders real flower species as hyper-refractive crystal/glass sculptures, presented as a digital "species catalog" — nav items Collection / Species / Journal / Gallery all reinforce a cataloguing, almost natural-history-museum tone crossed with high-luxury product photography. Overall mood: nocturnal, glacial, precious-gemstone botany — dark, cold, refined, still.

## Typography
Elegant, high-contrast, editorial pairing of a display serif with a soft geometric sans — classic luxury-botanical styling.
- **Display serif** (headline "CRYSTAL LOTUS", card title "CRAFTED IN CRYSTAL", and the big stat numerals "164"/"84"): a thin, high-contrast, transitional/didone-adjacent serif (visual family similar to Canela, Freight Big Pro, or Playfair Display) — thin hairline strokes, sharp bracketed serifs, elegant old-style numeral shapes on the stat figures. Set large (headline reads as roughly 60–72px equivalent at this frame size), tight-to-normal letter tracking, mixed case for the two-line headline, full-caps with wide tracking for "CRAFTED IN CRYSTAL".
- **Rounded/geometric sans** (everything else — brand wordmark "CHRYSANT", nav labels Home/Collection/Species/Journal/Gallery, numbered index "01"/"02", body paragraphs, "Know more" button label, stat unit superscripts "lux"/"cm" and stat labels "Light Exposure"/"Average Height", curved "SCROLL DOWN" label): a clean, soft-terminal grotesk (visual family similar to General Sans, Switzer, or Articulat CF) — single-story lowercase letterforms, generous x-height, small size (~11–14px equivalent) for UI chrome, slightly larger (~15–16px) for the body paragraph under "Frost Dahlia". Brand wordmark specifically is bold, all-caps, letter-spaced.
- Overall pairing logic: serif = the flower/brand's "precious object" identity; sans = the "catalog/interface" chrome around it.

## Motion — every element
- **Entrance (glass panel scales in)**: the sequence opens with just an empty, semi-transparent glass rectangle sitting over the oversized blurred backdrop — no text, no chips, just the crystal flower showing through the tinted glass pane. Content then fades in in a staggered cascade, not all at once: (1) brand wordmark + nav row fade in first, (2) headline "CRYSTAL LOTUS" + numbered item "01 Frost Dahlia" + body copy + "Know more" pill fade/settle in next, (3) the two floating stat chips fade in, (4) the "Crafted in Crystal" card + gallery thumbnails fade in last. Each element appears to fade up with a very slight upward settle rather than a hard cut.
- **Ambient / idle loop (crystal flower rotate/bloom, refraction shimmer)**: once settled, the crystal flower very subtly rotates/re-catches light — its facets shift specular highlights across the petal surfaces as if lit by a slowly moving key light, giving a persistent "shimmer" of refraction without the silhouette changing shape dramatically. No visible bloom/opening animation was captured (the flower stays fully bloomed throughout) — the "bloom" read is from light movement across the glass material, not petal growth.
- **Transitions**: the nav's "Home" item shows a pill-shaped capsule highlight behind its label, implying a sliding-capsule hover/active indicator that would slide between nav items on interaction (standard modern pill-nav pattern) — not confirmed moving in the captured frames, but strongly implied by the isolated rounded highlight only behind the active word.
- **Scroll / parallax / 3D tilt**: after the entrance settles, the camera performs a slow, continuous 3D orbit/tilt around the whole scene — pivoting from a flat front-on view (frames ~4–25) into an angled, tilted perspective (frames ~30–50, matching `t28-b.jpeg`) where the glass hero panel appears to recede in perspective like a floating pane in 3D space, and the crystal flower and "Crafted in Crystal" card loom larger/closer in the foreground. This reads as a camera-driven parallax move (or cursor-linked 3D tilt) rather than the UI elements themselves moving independently — foreground (flower, "Crafted in Crystal" card) shifts more than background text (headline, nav), producing real depth parallax.
- **Per-element motion (stat chips float)**: the two glass stat chips stay pinned in their screen-space column while the camera orbits/tilts, so relative to the rotating flower they read as gently floating/drifting cards — consistent with the template family's signature "glass stat chip with subtle idle float" treatment even though no independent bobbing motion beyond the camera parallax was distinguishable in the sampled frames.

## Reusable techniques
- **Oversized, heavily blurred duplicate of the hero subject as full-bleed backdrop** — creates depth and a "halo"/vignette without needing a second asset; cheap to reproduce with a large blurred/duplicated + darkened copy of the hero 3D render behind the sharp UI layer.
- **Glass/frosted rounded-rect panel as the entire hero container** — nav, headline, list, and CTA all live inside one translucent glass card rather than directly on the page background, giving a "product displayed under glass" feel that reinforces the "crystal" brand concept.
- **Numbered accordion-style feature list** ("01 label + body + CTA", followed by collapsed "02") — lets one hero panel imply a whole catalog without listing everything at once.
- **Floating glass stat chips with big serif numeral + small unit + sans label** — a compact, reusable "metric card" molecule (numeral, unit, label) that reads as premium/technical data rather than plain marketing copy.
- **Circular "SCROLL DOWN" badge with curved text + arrow** — a self-contained, on-brand scroll affordance that doubles as a decorative emblem (dashed ring reads like a wax-seal/coin).
- **Camera-driven 3D tilt/orbit over a static composition** — sells "3D-ness" and interactivity using a single rendered scene and a moving virtual camera, rather than requiring the 3D object itself to have complex rigged animation.
- **Serif-for-precious-object / sans-for-interface type pairing** — a fast, legible way to signal "luxury object, modern catalog" simultaneously.
